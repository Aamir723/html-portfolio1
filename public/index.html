Green Mile

In 1998, senior Paul Edgecomb lives in a retirement home. He suddenly starts crying on community television when Fred Astaire sings the song Cheek to Cheek in the film I Dance Myself into Your Heart. Paul tells his sympathetic roommate and friend Elaine why this film touches him so much. His memories go back to the 1930s. At the time, Paul was 44 years old and the head of death row at Cold Mountain, Louisiana, who was nicknamed "the Green Mile" because of the color of his linoleumfloor. Together with his colleagues, Paul had the task of guarding those sentenced to death and carrying out their execution in the electric chair. In 1935, a new prisoner, John Coffey, was transferred to death row. Coffey is a six-meter-tall, extremely muscular and scarred African-American who has been convicted of rape and murder of two little white girls. He doesn't seem very intelligent and speaks only in short and simple sentences.
